About Us

Joint Task Force Green

Combined Special Operations Joint Task Force 315 (CSOJTF-315), popularly known as Joint Task Force Green (JTFG) is a multinational UN force tasked with a combined mission set. The main purpose of the unit in place of any one Tier One or Tier Two SOF unit is to combine the experience and training of multiple forces to create a breeding ground for better training, tactics, and more successful missions. The unit originally formed with elements of the 22nd SAS Regiment in 2015 saw massive expansion and has since seen inclusion of Military, and Federal Police units from every country in the United Nations to the task force, leading to the Task Force being given the rare capability of operating both in a domestic and foreign role in multiple nations, this being due to the fact the task force was temporarily attached to the requisite agency and unit to the country prior to the deployment. Operators from Joint Task Force Green, which is made up of other units who operate on their own time act as their own force and deploy as an on call force, or to pre-planned combat deployments. Joint Task Force Green has deployed to Africa 11 times, Iraq and Syria 9 times, and Afghanistan 6 times, maintaining a very high operational tempo, they have lost 3 members in combat in their 7 year span and are virtually unknown to those outside of the SOF community, with members signing waivers agreeing to deny any knowledge of the unit for 20 years following their contract with the Armed Forces ending.
